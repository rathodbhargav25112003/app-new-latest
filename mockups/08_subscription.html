Gastro-oesophageal reflux disease

Definition. Pathological reflux of gastric contents into the oesophagus causing troublesome symptoms or complications.

Pathophysiology. Transient LOS relaxation is the commonest mechanism. Hiatus hernia, obesity and delayed gastric emptying all contribute.

Fig 9.2 · LOS anatomy

Clinical features. Heartburn and regurgitation are cardinal; dysphagia, chronic cough, laryngitis and dental erosion are extra-oesophageal presentations.

Key fact Long-standing GORD raises the risk of Barrett's oesophagus and adenocarcinoma — surveillance at 3-yearly intervals.

Vol II · Upper GI8

Investigations

Upper GI endoscopy. First-line when alarm features are present — dysphagia, weight loss, GI bleeding, age > 55.

pH studies. 24-hour ambulatory pH monitoring quantifies acid exposure time; the gold standard for diagnosis when symptoms are atypical.

Fig 9.3 · 24-h pH tracing

Manometry. Excludes motility disorders (e.g. achalasia) before anti-reflux surgery. LOS tone and peristalsis are mapped.

Barium swallow. Limited role now, but still useful to outline hiatus hernia anatomy before operative planning.

✨ Why one store beats two

One cart, one invoice

Students don't repeat their address or payment for books vs plans. Single GST-compliant invoice.

Preview sells books

A browser flipbook removes the #1 objection — "I can't see what I'm buying." Admins set preview range per volume.

Bundle magic

The engine understands children — the 5-volume set inherits stock from its five SKUs automatically.

Digital doubles printed

Plus subscribers read the matching e-book while waiting for the printed copy. Zero extra asset management.

Admins ship from one screen

Pick-list, label print, waybill push and status notifications all live in the same order drawer.

Low-stock alerts

Inventory crosses threshold → a banner on the SKU page + a Slack webhook to the warehouse lead.
